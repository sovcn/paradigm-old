Hello, and welcome to my website. I am a 21 year old computer science and math dual major studying at the University of Slippery Rock in Pennsylvania. My interests include: hiking, mountaineering, traveling, gaming, photography, open source, web design and development, rapid prototyping, user experience design, and software engineering.

When I was a freshmen in high school I started teaching myself Photosohp so that I could compete in weekly graphics design competitions in my gaming community (we called them clans). As I became more and more proficient with Photoshop, I found that it lended itself extremely well to web design and development. I soon decided that I wanted to make my own web platform, so I taught myself C++, PHP, and MySQL and started building a content management system for gaming communities on Battle.net. I have since released this project as open source on github, and it can be viewed here. In addition, I also worked on several other projects before graduating high school, most of which can be found in my portfolio.

Upon entering Slippery Rock University, I began my formal education as a computer scientist. I have since produced three research projects, all of which have been accepted into conferences. My most recent research, Platform Independent Cloud Scripting, won first place at the 2012 international ACM SIGCSE student research competition in March.

In my spare time, I love to travel and see the world. I lived in England for four months while studying at the University of Bradford, and I have spent time in Bermuda, Mexico, Belgium, Germany, France, and Spain. When I'm not traveling, I usually spend my time hiking, playing Starcraft II, or working on my various hobby projects. I am also an avid aquarist and fish keeper.
